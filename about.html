About Project Zion

Project Zion was born from a shared vision of creating a safer digital world for everyone. In an era where cybercrime is becoming increasingly sophisticated and victims often feel helpless and alone, we recognized the urgent need for a supportive, anonymous platform where people could seek help without fear of judgment or further exposure.

Our Origin Story

Project Zion emerged during the TENET'25 Hackathon, where our diverse team came together with a common goal: to combat the growing threat of digital crime and cyberbullying. As we researched the landscape of cybersecurity support, we discovered a troubling gap in accessible, anonymous assistance for victims of digital crimes.

Traditional reporting mechanisms often require extensive personal information, lengthy procedures, and can be intimidating for victims who are already traumatized. Many people suffer in silence, unsure of where to turn or afraid that seeking help might make their situation worse.

The Team's Motivation

Our diverse team brings together expertise in cybersecurity, mental health advocacy, user experience design, and social justice. We've all witnessed firsthand how digital crimes can devastate lives, and we're committed to ensuring that no one has to face these challenges alone.
